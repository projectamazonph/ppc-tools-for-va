# Amazon PPC Quiz Bank

> 50+ quiz questions across 5 difficulty levels. Use for self-assessment, coaching checkpoints, and interview prep.

## Level 1: Beginner (Fundamentals)

### Match Types
Q1. What are the three keyword match types in Amazon PPC?
**A:** Broad, Phrase, and Exact match.

Q2. A customer searches "wireless bluetooth headphones." Your keyword is "bluetooth headphones" set to **Phrase match**. Will your ad show?
**A:** Yes — phrase match allows additional words before or after the phrase, as long as the exact phrase is included in order.

Q3. Which match type gives you the MOST control over which searches trigger your ad?
**A:** Exact match — only triggers when the customer's search term matches the keyword exactly (or close variants).

Q4. Which match type gives you the WIDEST reach but least control?
**A:** Broad match — triggers for related searches including misspellings, synonyms, and related terms.

Q5. You're running an auto campaign. Where does Amazon pull keywords from?
**A:** The product listing — title, bullet points, description, and backend search terms.

### Campaign Types
Q6. What are the three main Amazon ad types?
**A:** Sponsored Products (SP), Sponsored Brands (SB), and Sponsored Display (SD).

Q7. Which ad type is available to ALL sellers (no Brand Registry needed)?
**A:** Sponsored Products.

Q8. Which ad type allows you to showcase your brand logo, custom headline, and multiple products?
**A:** Sponsored Brands.

Q9. Sponsored Display ads can appear:
**A:** On Amazon product detail pages, search results, and OFF Amazon on third-party websites and apps.

Q10. What is the difference between Product Targeting and Keyword Targeting?
**A:** Product Targeting shows your ads on specific ASINs or categories. Keyword Targeting shows your ads when customers search for specific keywords.

### Metrics
Q11. What does ACoS stand for?
**A:** Advertising Cost of Sale — the percentage of ad spend relative to ad revenue. Formula: (Ad Spend ÷ Ad Revenue) × 100.

Q12. If you spent $50 on ads and generated $200 in sales, what is your ACoS?
**A:** 25% ($50 ÷ $200 × 100).

Q13. What does ROAS stand for?
**A:** Return on Ad Spend — the revenue generated per dollar spent on ads. Formula: Ad Revenue ÷ Ad Spend.

Q14. Using the same numbers ($50 spend, $200 revenue), what is the ROAS?
**A:** 4.0x ($200 ÷ $50).

Q15. What is CPC?
**A:** Cost Per Click — the average amount you pay each time a customer clicks your ad. Formula: Total Ad Spend ÷ Total Clicks.

Q16. What is CTR?
**A:** Click-Through Rate — the percentage of impressions that resulted in a click. Formula: (Clicks ÷ Impressions) × 100.

Q17. What is a "good" ACoS for most sellers?
**A:** It depends on profit margin. Target ACoS = Break-even ACoS. If your margin is 30%, your target ACoS is ≤30%.

## Level 2: Intermediate (Optimization)

### Search Term Analysis
Q18. You download a Search Term Report and see a term with 20 clicks, 0 orders, and $40 in spend. What do you do?
**A:** Add it as a negative keyword — it's consuming budget with zero conversions.

Q19. You see a search term with 5 clicks, 3 orders, and a 60% conversion rate, but it's in an auto campaign. What's your next move?
**A:** Harvest it — add it as an exact match keyword in a manual campaign with an appropriate bid.

Q20. What is the "4-quadrant" framework for search term analysis?
**A:** 
- High clicks, High orders → Keep/optimize bid
- High clicks, Low orders → Investigate listing/negate
- Low clicks, High orders → Increase bid for more data
- Low clicks, Low orders → Monitor, negate if spend exceeds threshold

Q21. How often should you review search term reports?
**A:** Weekly for active campaigns. Daily during launch phase or high-spend periods.

Q22. What is "keyword harvesting"?
**A:** The process of extracting converting search terms from auto/automatic campaigns and adding them as exact match keywords in manual campaigns.

### Bid Optimization
Q23. A keyword has a high ACoS (45%) but is driving 30% of your total sales. Should you pause it?
**A:** No — reduce the bid gradually (10-15% at a time) to improve ACoS while maintaining volume. Pausing would lose significant sales.

Q24. What is "dynamic bidding" on Amazon?
**A:** Amazon's bid adjustment feature — "Down only" lowers bids when a click is less likely to convert; "Up and Down" raises bids (up to 100%) for high-conversion opportunities and lowers for low ones.

Q25. When should you use "Down Only" vs "Up and Down" bidding?
**A:** Down Only = safer, conservative. Up and Down = more aggressive, better for proven campaigns with conversion data.

Q26. You notice CPC has increased 20% over the past month while ACoS stayed the same. What does this indicate?
**A:** Competition increased (higher CPC), but your conversion rate also improved, keeping ACoS stable. Monitor — if CPC keeps rising, you may need to optimize listings or adjust strategy.

### Budget Management
Q27. What is the 70/20/10 budget allocation rule?
**A:** 70% to proven winners (exact match, high-converting), 20% to testing/expanding (phrase, broad), 10% to experimental (new keywords, auto campaigns).

Q28. Your campaign hits its daily budget by 2 PM every day. What should you do?
**A:** Three options: (1) Increase budget if ACoS is profitable, (2) Lower bids to stretch budget, (3) Adjust dayparting if certain hours convert better.

Q29. How do you calculate break-even ACoS?
**A:** Break-even ACoS = Profit Margin %. If your product sells for $30 and costs $18 to make/ship, margin = 40%, so break-even ACoS = 40%.

Q30. A campaign has ACoS of 15% and is generating strong sales. The client wants to scale. What's your approach?
**A:** Gradually increase budget by 20-30% every 3-5 days while monitoring ACoS. Add new keywords from search term report. Expand to phrase/broad match for discovery.

## Level 3: Advanced (Strategy)

### Campaign Structure
Q31. Explain the difference between "single keyword" and "multi-keyword" campaign structures.
**A:** Single keyword campaigns give maximum control per keyword (one bid, one match type per ad group). Multi-keyword campaigns group related keywords for easier management but less granular control. Best practice: single keyword exact match for top performers, multi-keyword for discovery.

Q32. What is a "portfolio" in Amazon Advertising?
**A:** A organizational container that groups campaigns together. Used for budget allocation, reporting by category/brand, and campaign organization.

Q33. You inherit an account with 50 auto campaigns and zero manual campaigns. Walk through your restructuring approach.
**A:** 
1. Audit all auto campaigns (2 weeks of data minimum)
2. Identify top converting search terms from each
3. Create manual campaigns: exact match for proven winners, phrase/broad for discovery
4. Add negative keywords to auto campaigns to prevent cannibalization
5. Archive underperforming auto campaigns after transition
6. Monitor for 2-4 weeks before further changes

Q34. What is "campaign cannibalization"?
**A:** When multiple campaigns bid on the same keyword, causing them to compete against each other, driving up CPC and reducing efficiency.

### Advanced Metrics
Q35. What is TACoS and why does it matter more than ACoS?
**A:** Total ACoS = Ad Spend ÷ Total Sales (organic + ad). It measures ad impact on overall business, not just ad-attributed sales. ACoS can be low while TACoS is high if organic sales are declining.

Q36. You see ACoS decreasing but TACoS increasing. What does this mean?
**A:** Ads are getting more efficient, but organic sales are declining — possibly because you reduced ad spend too aggressively, losing organic ranking momentum.

Q37. What is "halo effect" in Amazon PPC?
**A:** When PPC advertising drives organic ranking improvements — increased sales velocity from ads leads to better organic positions, reducing overall ACoS/TACoS over time.

Q38. How do you calculate the true profitability of a PPC campaign including halo effect?
**A:** Factor in: (1) Direct ad-attributed revenue, (2) Estimated organic lift from increased sales velocity, (3) Improved organic ranking value, (4) Brand awareness/retargeting value.

### Negative Keywords
Q39. What's the difference between negative exact and negative phrase?
**A:** Negative exact blocks only the exact search term. Negative phrase blocks any search containing that phrase.

Q40. When should you negate at campaign level vs. account level?
**A:** Campaign level: when a term is irrelevant only for that specific campaign/product. Account level: when a term is universally irrelevant across all products (e.g., "free," "cheap," "DIY").

## Level 4: Expert (Client Management)

Q41. A client says "My ACoS is 50%, that's terrible — pause everything." How do you respond?
**A:** First, assess: (1) Is 50% above or below break-even? (2) Are organic sales increasing from ad-driven velocity? (3) Is the account in launch phase (higher ACoS expected)? Present data showing total business impact, not just ACoS. Recommend optimization, not pausing.

Q42. A new client has never run ads. Their product is $25, cost is $8, and they want ACoS under 20%. Is this realistic?
**A:** Break-even ACoS = 68% ($17 margin ÷ $25 price). Under 20% is very aggressive for a new product with no reviews/data. Recommend: start at 40-50% ACoS to build data, then optimize down. Set expectations that 20% may take 3-6 months.

Q43. How do you handle a client who wants to micromanage every bid change?
**A:** (1) Set clear reporting cadence (weekly). (2) Explain the "why" behind changes. (3) Show before/after results. (4) Establish decision thresholds ("I'll adjust bids when ACoS changes by >10%"). (5) Build trust through transparency.

Q44. A competitor is bidding on your brand terms. What's your defense strategy?
**A:** (1) Launch a dedicated brand defense campaign (exact match, brand keywords). (2) Set competitive bids to maintain top position. (3) Use Sponsored Brands for brand SERP dominance. (4) Monitor competitor activity weekly. (5) Consider legal action if trademark infringement.

Q45. How do you explain PPC performance to a non-technical client?
**A:** Use analogies: "Think of PPC like a storefront on a busy street. We're paying rent (ad spend) to be visible. Our goal is to make sure the people walking by are actually looking for what we sell (targeting), and once they see us, they want to buy (listing optimization)."

## Level 5: Scenario-Based (Problem Solving)

Q46. SCENARIO: You launch a new product with no reviews. Ads are running but CTR is 0.1% (very low). What's wrong and how do you fix it?
**A:** Likely causes: (1) Low review count deters clicks. (2) Main image not compelling. (3) Price not competitive. Fix: Optimize main image, ensure competitive pricing, enroll in Vine for reviews, use Sponsored Brands video for visual appeal, consider coupon/promotion.

Q47. SCENARIO: A product was profitable at 25% ACoS. Suddenly ACoS jumps to 55% with no changes on your end. Investigate.
**A:** Check: (1) New competitor entered and bidding aggressively? (2) Seasonal demand shift? (3) Amazon changed auction dynamics? (4) Listing suppressed or out of stock? (5) Review count dropped? (6) CPC inflation in category? Action: Review search term report for new expensive terms, check competitor count, verify listing status.

Q48. SCENARIO: You have $500/month budget across 10 products. How do you allocate?
**A:** Prioritize: (1) Top 3 best sellers get 60% ($300). (2) Next 3 growth products get 30% ($150). (3) Remaining 4 get 10% ($50) for testing. Focus spend where conversion data exists. Don't spread too thin — better to dominate 3 products than weakly support 10.

Q49. SCENARIO: Client wants to launch during Q4 (Black Friday/Cyber Monday). Budget is $5,000 for November-December. Plan your approach.
**A:** 
- October: Build campaign structure, keyword research, bid baselines
- Nov 1-15: Ramp up spend 20% weekly, build data
- Nov 16-24: Aggressive bids (+30-50%) for Black Friday week
- Nov 25-Dec 1: Maintain high bids, monitor ACoS hourly
- Dec 2-15: Scale back to sustainable bids, harvest winning terms
- Dec 16-31: Holiday maintenance, reduce experimental spend

Q50. SCENARIO: You're managing 3 accounts simultaneously. How do you structure your week?
**A:** 
- Monday: Account 1 deep dive (search terms, bids, budgets)
- Tuesday: Account 2 deep dive
- Wednesday: Account 3 deep dive
- Thursday: Reporting, client calls, strategy adjustments
- Friday: Weekly review, next week planning, new keyword research
- Daily: 15-min health check on all accounts (budget pacing, anomalies)

## Quiz Format for Coaching Use

### Quick-Fire Round (5 min)
Give student 10 questions, 30 seconds each. Score: 8+/10 = pass.

### Written Assessment (30 min)
25 questions, mix of multiple choice and short answer. Score: 80%+ = competency verified.

### Live Scenario Test (15 min)
Present a real account screenshot. Student must identify 3 issues and propose solutions.

### Weekly Knowledge Check (5 questions)
Rotating topics. Use in Facebook group or coaching sessions.
