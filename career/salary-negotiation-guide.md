## Freelance Rate Calculator

### Hourly to Monthly Conversion

```
Monthly Salary = Hourly Rate × Hours per Week × 4.33

Example:
$20/hr × 40 hrs/week × 4.33 = $3,464/month
```

### Monthly to Hourly Conversion

```
Hourly Rate = Monthly Salary / (Hours per Week × 4.33)

Example:
₱50,000 / (40 × 4.33) = ₱289/hr
```

## When to Ask for a Raise

### Ask When:
1. ✅ You've been in the role 6+ months
2. ✅ You have documented results (ACoS improvement, revenue growth)
3. ✅ You've taken on more responsibility
4. ✅ Market rates have increased
5. ✅ Client's business has grown (you helped them grow)
6. ✅ You have competing offers

### Don't Ask When:
1. ❌ You just started (< 3 months)
2. ❌ You made a recent mistake
3. ❌ Client is in financial trouble
4. ❌ You haven't documented your value
5. ❌ You're emotional or desperate

## How to Ask for a Raise

### Step 1: Document Your Value

Create a "Value Document" with:

```
MY CONTRIBUTIONS (Last 6 Months)

1. ACoS Improvement
   • Before: [X]%
   • After: [X]%
   • Savings: $[X]/month

2. Revenue Growth
   • Before: $[X]/month
   • After: $[X]/month
   • Increase: [X]%

3. Time Savings
   • Automated [X] tasks
   • Saved [X] hours/week
   • Value: $[X]/month

4. Additional Responsibilities
   • [Task you took on]
   • [Task you took on]

5. Client Satisfaction
   • [Testimonial or positive feedback]
   • [Retention period]
```

### Step 2: Research Market Rate

- Check OnlineJobs.ph for current rates
- Search Upwork for similar roles
- Ask peers in VA communities
- Note: "Market rate for my skill level is ₱[X]"

### Step 3: Make the Ask

**Email Template:**

```
Subject: Performance Review & Compensation Discussion

Hi [Client Name],

I wanted to schedule a brief call to discuss my performance and 
compensation. Over the past [X] months, I've:

• Reduced ACoS from [X]% to [X]%, saving $[X]/month
• Increased revenue by [X]% ($[X] → $[X]/month)
• [Additional achievement]

Based on these results and current market rates for PPC specialists 
with my experience level, I'd like to discuss adjusting my rate 
from [current] to [proposed].

I'm committed to continuing to deliver strong results for [Company] 
and would love to discuss this further.

Are you available for a quick call this week?

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### Step 4: Handle the Response

**If Yes:**
- Thank them
- Get the new rate in writing
- Continue delivering results

**If No:**
- Ask: "What would I need to do to earn a raise?"
- Set clear milestones
- Review in 3-6 months

**If "Not Now":**
- Ask: "When would be a good time to revisit?"
- Set a calendar reminder
- Document the conversation

## Rate Negotiation Scripts

### For New Clients

**Client asks: "What's your rate?"**

❌ Bad: "Whatever you think is fair"
✅ Good: "My rate for PPC management is $[X]/month for accounts up to $[X] monthly ad spend. This includes weekly reporting, bid optimization, and search term analysis. For larger accounts, I offer custom pricing. What's your current ad spend?"

### For Rate Increase

**You say:**

"I've been managing your account for [X] months now, and I wanted to share some results:
- ACoS reduced from [X]% to [X]%
- Revenue increased by [X]%
- [Other achievement]

Given these results and my growing expertise, I'd like to adjust my rate from [current] to [proposed], effective [date]. I believe this reflects the value I'm delivering and current market rates."

### For Project Pricing

**Client asks: "How much for a full audit?"**

"My PPC audit includes:
- Full account review (campaigns, keywords, bids, structure)
- Search term analysis
- Competitive landscape review
- Detailed recommendations report
- 30-minute strategy call

The investment is $[X]. Most clients see ROI within 60 days of implementing recommendations. Shall I proceed?"

## Common Pricing Mistakes

### ❌ Don't Do This
1. **Quoting too low** — "I'll do it for $200/month" (undermines your value)
2. **Hourly billing for ongoing work** — Creates adversarial relationship
3. **No contract** — Leaves you unprotected
4. **Free trial period** — Attracts tire-kickers
5. **Competing on price** — Race to the bottom

### ✅ Do This Instead
1. **Value-based pricing** — Price based on results you deliver
2. **Monthly retainer** — Predictable income, predictable service
3. **Clear scope of work** — Both parties know what's included
4. **Contract with terms** — 30-day notice, payment terms
5. **Competing on expertise** — "I reduce ACoS by 30%" not "I'm cheap"
