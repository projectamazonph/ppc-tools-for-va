# Amazon PPC Interview Question Bank

> 60+ real interview questions for Amazon PPC Specialist roles. Organized by competency. Include ideal answer guides for coaching.

---

## Category 1: Technical Knowledge (15 questions)

### Q1: Walk me through the three Amazon ad types and when you'd use each.
**Ideal Answer:** Sponsored Products (SP) — individual product listings, available to all sellers, best for direct sales. Sponsored Brands (SB) — brand headline + logo + multiple products, requires Brand Registry, best for brand awareness and storefront traffic. Sponsored Display (SD) — retargeting and audience targeting, appears on and off Amazon, best for conquesting and remarketing.

### Q2: Explain the difference between Broad, Phrase, and Exact match types with examples.
**Ideal Answer:** Broad — widest reach, triggers for related terms (e.g., "bluetooth headphones" triggers for "wireless audio headset"). Phrase — exact phrase must appear in order, can have words before/after (e.g., "bluetooth headphones" triggers for "best bluetooth headphones 2026"). Exact — only triggers for the exact term or close variants (e.g., "bluetooth headphones" triggers for "bluetooth headphone" but not "wireless bluetooth headset").

### Q3: How do you calculate ACoS and ROAS? Give me a real example.
**Ideal Answer:** ACoS = (Ad Spend ÷ Ad Revenue) × 100. ROAS = Ad Revenue ÷ Ad Spend. Example: Spent $200, generated $800 in sales. ACoS = 25%, ROAS = 4.0x. If margin is 35%, we're profitable (ACoS < margin).

### Q4: What is TACoS and why do you prefer it over ACoS for measuring overall performance?
**Ideal Answer:** TACoS = Ad Spend ÷ Total Sales (organic + ad-attributed). It shows how ads impact the entire business, not just ad sales. ACoS can look great while organic sales tank. TACoS reveals the true picture — a declining TACoS means ads are fueling organic growth.

### Q5: A keyword has 50 clicks, 2 orders, and ACoS of 80%. The product margin is 35%. What do you do?
**Ideal Answer:** 80% ACoS is above break-even (35%), so this keyword is losing money. But 2 orders in 50 clicks = 4% CVR — not terrible. Options: (1) Reduce bid by 20-30% to improve ACoS. (2) Check if the listing needs optimization for this keyword. (3) If after 2 weeks ACoS doesn't improve, negate it. Don't pause immediately — gather more data.

### Q6: What's the difference between Auto and Manual campaigns?
**Ideal Answer:** Auto campaigns let Amazon match your ads to relevant search terms using your listing data. Manual campaigns let you choose specific keywords and bids. Auto = discovery (finds new terms). Manual = control (targets proven terms). Best practice: run both — auto for harvesting, manual for targeting.

### Q7: Explain how Amazon's auction system works for ad placement.
**Ideal Answer:** Amazon uses a second-price auction. You set a max bid, but you typically pay just enough to beat the next competitor. Ad Rank = Bid × Relevance Score (CTR, conversion rate, listing quality). Higher relevance can mean lower CPC for the same placement.

### Q8: What are product targeting ads vs. keyword targeting ads?
**Ideal Answer:** Product targeting shows your ad on specific ASIN detail pages or category pages. Keyword targeting shows your ad when customers search specific terms. Product targeting = competitor conquesting, category browsing. Keyword targeting = search intent capture.

### Q9: How does Amazon Attribution work?
**Ideal Answer:** Amazon Attribution tracks off-Amazon marketing (Google Ads, Facebook, email) and shows how those channels drive Amazon sales. It provides referral traffic, detail page views, add-to-carts, and purchase metrics for non-Amazon channels.

### Q10: What is Day Parting and when would you use it?
**Ideal Answer:** Day Parting = scheduling ads to run during specific hours/days when conversion rates are highest. Use when: (1) Data shows clear hourly conversion patterns. (2) Budget is limited and you want to concentrate spend during peak hours. (3) Competitor activity is lower during off-hours.

### Q11: Explain the difference between Placement adjustments and Bid adjustments.
**Ideal Answer:** Placement adjustments increase your bid for specific placements (Top of Search, Product Pages, Rest of Search). Bid adjustments are per-keyword bid changes. Placement = "show me more here" (percentage increase). Bid = "this keyword is worth more/less" (dollar amount).

### Q12: What is the "New Customer Metric" in Amazon Ads?
**Ideal Answer:** Amazon's metric showing how many purchases came from customers who haven't bought from your brand before. Useful for measuring brand expansion vs. repeat purchases. Important for new product launches and brand growth strategies.

### Q13: How do you handle a campaign with high impressions but very low CTR?
**Ideal Answer:** Low CTR with high impressions = ad is showing but not getting clicked. Causes: (1) Main image not compelling. (2) Title not relevant to search term. (3) Price not competitive. (4) Low review count. (5) Wrong targeting. Fix: optimize listing first, then check keyword relevance, then adjust targeting.

### Q14: What is the "Impression Share" and how do you improve it?
**Ideal Answer:** Impression Share = your impressions ÷ total available impressions for your keywords. Low impression share means you're missing visibility. Improve: (1) Increase bids. (2) Improve Quality Score (CTR, relevance). (3) Expand keyword list. (4) Increase budgets.

### Q15: Explain how Sponsored Brands Video differs from regular SP ads.
**Ideal Answer:** SB Video auto-plays in search results, stops the scroll, and tells a story. Requires Brand Registry. Better for: (1) Products needing demonstration. (2) Brand storytelling. (3) Competitive categories where static images blend in. Typically higher CTR than static SP ads.

## Category 2: Scenario-Based (15 questions)

### Q16: A client's ACoS jumped from 25% to 60% in one week. Walk me through your investigation.
**Ideal Answer:** (1) Check for new competitors bidding on same keywords. (2) Review search term report for new expensive terms. (3) Check if product is out of stock or suppressed. (4) Look for seasonal demand shifts. (5) Verify listing hasn't changed (title, image, price). (6) Check if Amazon changed auction dynamics. (7) Review budget — did campaign hit daily budget early?

### Q17: You're launching a new product with zero reviews. How do you approach PPC?
**Ideal Answer:** (1) Accept higher ACoS initially (40-60%) — building data costs money. (2) Start with auto campaign for discovery. (3) Add manual exact match for top 5-10 keywords. (4) Use Sponsored Brands video for visual appeal. (5) Enroll in Vine for reviews. (6) Optimize listing before launching ads. (7) Set client expectations — profitability takes 4-8 weeks.

### Q18: A product has great ACoS (15%) but organic sales are declining. What's happening?
**Ideal Answer:** Likely: too much reliance on PPC, not enough organic ranking investment. TACoS may be rising even though ACoS is low. Fix: (1) Increase PPC spend to drive more velocity → organic ranking lift. (2) Optimize listing for organic ranking. (3) Run PPC at slight loss to build organic rank, then scale back.

### Q19: You have $300/month budget across 8 products. How do you allocate?
**Ideal Answer:** Don't spread thin. (1) Identify top 2-3 products by conversion rate and margin. (2) Allocate 70% to those winners ($210). (3) 20% to growth products ($60). (4) 10% to testing ($30). (5) If a product can't convert with $30/month, pause it and focus on winners.

### Q20: A competitor is running ads on your brand name. How do you respond?
**Ideal Answer:** (1) Launch brand defense campaign — exact match on brand terms, competitive bids. (2) Use Sponsored Brands for brand SERP ownership. (3) Monitor competitor ad copy for trademark issues. (4) Ensure brand content is optimized (A+ Content, Storefront). (5) Consider legal action if they're using your trademarked name in ad copy.

### Q21: Your best-performing keyword suddenly has 0 impressions. Investigate.
**Ideal Answer:** (1) Check if product is out of stock. (2) Check if keyword bid is too low (competition may have increased). (3) Check if campaign is paused or budget exhausted. (4) Check if Amazon changed keyword relevance. (5) Check if ASIN is suppressed. (6) Try increasing bid by 20-30% to test.

### Q22: Client wants to cut PPC budget by 50% immediately. What's your response?
**Ideal Answer:** (1) Show TACoS impact — cutting ads may hurt organic ranking. (2) Identify which campaigns are most efficient. (3) Propose: cut underperformers first, maintain winners. (4) Set expectation: organic sales will likely drop 20-40% within 2-4 weeks. (5) If they insist, cut strategically: keep brand defense, cut discovery.

### Q23: You notice a keyword converting at 20% but ACoS is 45% (above break-even). What do you do?
**Ideal Answer:** High CVR + high ACoS = bid is too high. (1) Reduce bid by 15-20%. (2) Monitor — if ACoS drops below break-even while maintaining volume, great. (3) If volume drops significantly, bid was supporting placement — find the sweet spot. (4) Consider: is this keyword driving organic rank? If yes, accept slightly higher ACoS.

### Q24: A new client shows you their account — 200 campaigns, most with $1/day budget. Your reaction?
**Ideal Answer:** Classic mistake: too many campaigns, too little budget per campaign. Fix: (1) Consolidate — merge related campaigns. (2) Kill the dead ones (0 impressions in 30 days). (3) Focus budget on top 10-15 campaigns. (4) Minimum $10-20/day per campaign for meaningful data. (5) Restructure: auto → manual harvest flow.

### Q25: You inherit an account with 90% ACoS. The seller is losing money. What's your 30-day plan?
**Ideal Answer:**
- Week 1: Audit. Pause any campaign with >100% ACoS and <1% CVR. Identify quick wins (high CVR keywords with inflated bids).
- Week 2: Restructure. Create proper campaign hierarchy. Add negative keywords aggressively.
- Week 3: Optimize bids. Focus on keywords with 20-40% ACoS potential.
- Week 4: Scale what works, cut what doesn't. Target: 50-60% ACoS (still above break-even but trending down).

### Q26: A product is selling well organically but PPC ACoS is 70%. Should you keep running ads?
**Ideal Answer:** Depends on TACoS impact. (1) Calculate: is PPC driving organic ranking? If TACoS is declining, yes — ads are fueling organic growth. (2) If TACoS is stable/increasing, ads aren't helping organic — reduce spend. (3) Test: pause ads for 2 weeks, monitor organic sales. If organic drops, ads were supporting rank.

### Q27: Client asks: "Why is my CPC so high compared to competitors?"
**Ideal Answer:** CPC is determined by: (1) Competition level in your category. (2) Your Quality Score (CTR, relevance, conversion rate). (3) Your bid vs. competitor bids. (4) Placement targeting. Improve: optimize listing for higher CTR → better Quality Score → lower CPC. Don't just raise bids — improve relevance.

### Q28: You're managing Sponsored Brands and Sponsored Products for the same product. How do you prevent cannibalization?
**Ideal Answer:** (1) Use different keyword match types (SP = exact, SB = phrase/broad). (2) Use different targeting (SP = keywords, SB = product targeting). (3) Monitor search term overlap in reports. (4) Allocate budget by goal (SP = conversion, SB = awareness). (5) Accept some overlap — SB helps brand SERP dominance.

### Q29: A campaign has great ACoS but the client says sales aren't increasing. How do you diagnose?
**Ideal Answer:** (1) Check total sales (organic + ad). If total is flat, PPC isn't driving incremental sales — it's cannibalizing organic. (2) Check TACoS. (3) Check if ad-attributed sales are new customers or repeat. (4) Possible fix: expand to new keywords/products, not just optimize existing.

### Q30: It's October and client wants to prepare for Q4 (Black Friday). What's your plan?
**Ideal Answer:**
- October: Build campaign structure, keyword research, set bid baselines
- Early November: Ramp spend 20% weekly, gather conversion data
- Nov 16-24: Aggressive bids (+30-50%), increase budgets 2-3x
- Black Friday week: Monitor hourly, maintain top positions
- December: Scale back to sustainable levels, harvest winners
- Post-holiday: Analyze data, plan for next year

## Category 3: Tool Proficiency (10 questions)

### Q31: What tools do you use for Amazon PPC and why?
**Ideal Answer:** (1) Amazon Advertising Console — native campaign management. (2) Helium 10 — keyword research (Cerebro, Magnet), bid optimization (Adtomic). (3) Excel/Google Sheets — custom reporting, data analysis. (4) Google Analytics — off-Amazon traffic tracking. (5) Jungle Scout/Sellics — competitor analysis, market data.

### Q32: Walk me through how you use Helium 10's Cerebro for keyword research.
**Ideal Answer:** (1) Enter competitor ASIN. (2) Filter by search volume, relevance, number of results. (3) Identify keywords where competitor ranks organically but doesn't run ads. (4) Export list, prioritize by relevance and volume. (5) Add to manual campaigns with appropriate bids.

### Q33: How do you build a custom PPC report in Excel/Google Sheets?
**Ideal Answer:** (1) Pull data from Amazon Advertising API or manual download. (2) Create pivot tables by campaign, keyword, match type. (3) Calculate derived metrics (ACoS, ROAS, CVR, CPC). (4) Add conditional formatting for red/green flags. (5) Create charts for trend visualization. (6) Automate with scripts if possible.

### Q34: What's your process for using Amazon's Search Term Report?
**Ideal Answer:** (1) Download weekly (at minimum). (2) Sort by spend descending. (3) Identify: high-spend/low-conversion → negate. (4) Identify: high-conversion/low-impression → increase bid. (5) Identify: new converting terms → harvest to manual. (6) Track changes over time for trends.

### Q35: How do you use bulk operations in Amazon Ads?
**Ideal Answer:** (1) Download bulk operations template. (2) Make changes in Excel (bid adjustments, budget changes, campaign settings). (3) Upload modified file. (4) Review changes before submitting. Use for: mass bid adjustments, budget changes, campaign cloning, keyword additions across multiple campaigns.

### Q36: Describe your experience with Amazon Marketing Console (AMS) vs. standard Seller Central ads.
**Ideal Answer:** AMS (now Amazon Advertising Console) is the unified platform for all ad types. Seller Central has basic PPC. AMS/Console offers: all three ad types (SP, SB, SD), advanced targeting, bulk operations, portfolio management, detailed reporting, API access. Always use the Console for professional PPC management.

### Q37: How do you use Helium 10's Adtomic for bid automation?
**Ideal Answer:** (1) Set ACoS target per campaign. (2) Define rules: "If ACoS > target, reduce bid by X%." "If ACoS < target and orders > Y, increase bid by Z%." (3) Set guardrails: min/max bids, daily change limits. (4) Monitor weekly — automation isn't "set and forget." (5) Adjust rules based on performance trends.

### Q38: What's your approach to using Google Analytics for Amazon PPC?
**Ideal Answer:** (1) Set up Amazon Attribution for off-Amazon tracking. (2) Create UTM parameters for external traffic sources. (3) Track: click-through rate, detail page views, add-to-carts, purchases. (4) Use to measure ROI of external traffic driving to Amazon. (5) Compare: Amazon PPC vs. Google Ads vs. Facebook Ads performance.

### Q39: How do you handle bulk keyword negation across multiple campaigns?
**Ideal Answer:** (1) Compile negative keyword list from search term reports. (2) Use bulk operations template to add negatives in bulk. (3) Decide: campaign-level or shared negative keyword lists. (4) Shared lists = efficient for account-wide negatives. Campaign-level = specific to that campaign's scope. (5) Upload and verify.

### Q40: What reporting cadence do you follow and what do you include?
**Ideal Answer:**
- **Daily:** Budget pacing, anomalies, out-of-stock alerts (5 min)
- **Weekly:** Search term analysis, bid adjustments, performance trends (30 min)
- **Monthly:** Full performance review, client report, strategy adjustments (2 hrs)
- **Quarterly:** Strategic review, budget reallocation, competitive analysis (half day)

## Category 4: Client Communication (10 questions)

### Q41: How do you explain ACoS to a non-technical client?
**Ideal Answer:** "ACoS is like your return on investment for ads. If you spend $1 on ads and get $4 in sales, your ACoS is 25% — meaning 25 cents of every sales dollar went to advertising. Lower is generally better, but we need to balance low ACoS with growth."

### Q42: Client demands daily reports. How do you handle this?
**Ideal Answer:** (1) Explain that daily data is noisy — trends matter more. (2) Offer: daily automated dashboard (if available) for their peace of mind. (3) Commit to weekly detailed report + monthly strategic review. (4) Set up alerts for anomalies (budget depletion, ACoS spikes).

### Q43: A client is upset because ACoS increased last week. How do you respond?
**Ideal Answer:** (1) Acknowledge concern. (2) Investigate before responding — have data ready. (3) Explain context: "ACoS increased because [specific reason], but here's the positive: [total sales increased / organic ranking improved / new keywords are performing]." (4) Present action plan: "Here's what I'm doing to address it."

### Q44: How do you handle a client who wants to target keywords with zero search volume?
**Ideal Answer:** (1) Show search volume data — prove the keyword has no demand. (2) Explain: ads on zero-volume keywords = wasted spend. (3) Offer alternatives: related keywords with proven volume. (4) If they insist, run a small test ($20 budget) for 1 week, then show results.

### Q45: Describe your process for onboarding a new PPC client.
**Ideal Answer:** (1) Discovery call — goals, budget, timeline, expectations. (2) Account access (Seller Central + Advertising Console). (3) Full audit (1-2 weeks). (4) Present findings + strategy proposal. (5) Get approval. (6) Implement changes. (7) First report at 2 weeks. (8) Monthly reviews thereafter.

### Q46: How do you handle scope creep from a client?
**Ideal Answer:** (1) Define scope clearly in onboarding (what's included, what's extra). (2) When requests exceed scope: "That's a great idea. It falls outside our current agreement. I can add it for [X additional cost/time]. Shall I proceed?" (3) Document everything. (4) Never say "that's not my job" — say "let me add that to our scope."

### Q47: A client wants to pause all ads during slow season. Your advice?
**Ideal Answer:** (1) Don't pause everything — maintain brand defense (20-30% of normal budget). (2) Pause discovery/experimental campaigns. (3) Explain: pausing = losing organic rank momentum. (4) Suggest: reduce budget by 50-70%, not 100%. (5) Plan: ramp back up 4-6 weeks before peak season.

### Q48: How do you present a monthly performance report to a client?
**Ideal Answer:** Structure: (1) Executive summary (3 bullet points). (2) Key metrics vs. last month vs. target. (3) What worked (top campaigns, keywords). (4) What didn't (underperformers, actions taken). (5) Recommendations for next month. (6) Competitive landscape updates. Keep it visual — charts, not walls of text.

### Q49: Client asks "Why should I pay you when I can just use Adtomic?"
**Ideal Answer:** "Adtomic automates bids — I optimize strategy. Tools don't: understand your business goals, make judgment calls on budget allocation, handle client communication, adapt to market changes, or build long-term growth plans. I'm the strategist; the tool is the executor."

### Q50: How do you handle disagreements with a client about strategy?
**Ideal Answer:** (1) Listen fully — understand their concern. (2) Present data supporting your recommendation. (3) Acknowledge their perspective has merit. (4) Offer compromise: "Let's test both approaches with 20% of budget each, then let data decide." (5) Document the decision and outcome. (6) Never take it personally.

## Category 5: VA-Specific (10 questions)

### Q51: Why do you want to specialize in Amazon PPC instead of general VA work?
**Ideal Answer:** PPC is where the money is — specialists earn 2-3x more than general VAs. It's data-driven (clear metrics = clear value), scalable (skills transfer across clients), and in high demand. I want to build a career, not just a job.

### Q52: How do you manage your time across multiple Amazon accounts?
**Ideal Answer:** (1) Time-block: specific hours per account. (2) Daily 15-min health check per account. (3) Weekly deep dives per account. (4) Use SOPs for consistency. (5) Set up alerts for anomalies. (6) Batch similar tasks (all search term reports on Monday, all bid adjustments on Tuesday).

### Q53: What's your approach to learning new Amazon advertising features?
**Ideal Answer:** (1) Follow Amazon Ads blog and changelog. (2) Join PPC communities (Reddit r/AmazonPPC, Facebook groups). (3) Watch Helium 10/Sellics webinars. (4) Test new features on low-budget campaigns first. (5) Document learnings for future reference.

### Q54: How do you handle a situation where a client's product has negative reviews affecting PPC performance?
**Ideal Answer:** (1) Flag to client immediately — reviews impact CVR. (2) Recommend: address negative review themes (product issues, shipping, expectations). (3) Temporarily reduce PPC spend on affected product. (4) Focus spend on better-reviewed products. (5) Suggest: Vine program, follow-up emails, product improvements.

### Q55: Describe your daily routine as an Amazon PPC VA.
**Ideal Answer:**
- 8:00 AM: Check all accounts (budget pacing, anomalies, out-of-stock)
- 8:30 AM: Search term review — harvest winners, negate losers
- 9:30 AM: Bid adjustments based on yesterday's data
- 10:30 AM: New keyword research / campaign setup
- 11:30 AM: Client communication (emails, Slack)
- 1:00 PM: Reporting / documentation
- 2:00 PM: Deep optimization (A/B tests, campaign restructuring)
- 3:00 PM: Learning / community engagement

### Q56: A client gives you access to their account and you find 50+ campaigns with no structure. What do you do?
**Ideal Answer:** (1) Don't panic. (2) Take screenshots of everything before making changes. (3) Audit: identify which campaigns have data vs. which are dead. (4) Create a restructuring plan document. (5) Present to client: "Here's what I found, here's my proposed structure, here's the timeline." (6) Implement in phases — don't change everything at once.

### Q57: How do you stay organized with multiple clients and deadlines?
**Ideal Answer:** (1) Project management tool (Trello, Asana, or Notion). (2) Calendar with recurring tasks. (3) SOPs for every repeatable process. (4) Templates for reports and communications. (5) Daily prioritization (top 3 tasks per client). (6) Weekly review and planning.

### Q58: What do you do when you don't know the answer to a client's PPC question?
**Ideal Answer:** (1) Don't guess. (2) Say: "Great question — let me research that and get back to you by [specific time]." (3) Check: Amazon Ads documentation, Helium 10 resources, PPC communities. (4) If still uncertain: "Here's what I found, and here's my recommendation. Let's test it to confirm." (5) Document the answer for future reference.

### Q59: How do you handle a client who micromanages your work?
**Ideal Answer:** (1) Increase transparency — more frequent updates, detailed documentation. (2) Explain your process step-by-step. (3) Show results — data builds trust. (4) Set boundaries: "I'll update you every Monday and Thursday. For urgent issues, I'm available on Slack." (5) Be patient — trust takes time.

### Q60: What's the biggest mistake you see new PPC VAs make?
**Ideal Answer:** (1) Changing too much too fast — let data accumulate before optimizing. (2) Ignoring search term reports — this is where the gold is. (3) Not communicating with clients — silence creates anxiety. (4) Focusing on ACoS instead of total business impact (TACoS). (5) Not documenting changes — when something works, you need to know why.
