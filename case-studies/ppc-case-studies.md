## Case Study 1: Home & Lifestyle Brand — 102% Revenue Growth in 100 Days

**Source:** EcomManagers
**Category:** Home Decor
**Starting ACoS:** 42%
**Final ACoS:** 18%

### Before (The Problem)
- ACoS of 42% — actively eroding profitability
- Automatic campaigns only — no manual structure
- 60% of ad spend going to irrelevant/low-converting keywords
- Zero negative keywords — waste daily
- No performance tracking system
- No Sponsored Brands or Sponsored Display

### Strategy (The Fix)
**Phase 1 — Full Account Audit (Week 1)**
- Comprehensive audit of every active campaign
- Found: 60% of spend on keywords with zero conversions
- No separation between auto and manual campaigns
- Zero negative keywords at account level

**Phase 2 — Keyword Research & Segmentation (Week 2-3)**
- Used Amazon search term data + third-party tools
- Built manual exact match campaigns for proven converters
- Created phrase/broad campaigns for discovery
- Implemented negative keyword lists

**Phase 3 — Bid Optimization (Week 3-6)**
- Dynamic bidding: "Down Only" for proven campaigns, "Up and Down" for new ones
- Weekly bid adjustments based on 7-day rolling ACoS
- Placement adjustments: +50% for Top of Search on high-CVR keywords

**Phase 4 — Expansion (Week 6-12)**
- Launched Sponsored Brands campaigns
- Added Sponsored Display retargeting
- Built campaign portfolios by product category
- Implemented dayparting based on conversion data

### After (The Results)
| Metric | Before | After | Change |
|--------|--------|-------|--------|
| Revenue | Baseline | +102% | ⬆️ |
| ACoS | 42% | 18% | ⬇️ 57% |
| CTR | 0.3% | 0.8% | ⬆️ 167% |
| CVR | 8% | 14% | ⬆️ 75% |
| Ad Spend Efficiency | Poor | Strong | ⬆️ |

### Key Lessons
1. Structure matters — auto-only campaigns leave money on the table
2. Negative keywords are as important as positive keywords
3. Expansion (SB + SD) adds incremental revenue, not cannibalization
4. 100 days is realistic for significant improvement — don't expect overnight results

## Case Study 2: Supplement Brand — From 93% ACoS to Profitable

**Source:** VAA Philippines
**Category:** Supplements
**Starting ACoS:** 93%
**Final ACoS:** ~35%

### Before (The Problem)
- ACoS of 93% — spending nearly $1 for every $1 in revenue
- Multiple overlapping campaigns competing against each other
- Branded keywords buried in low-performing campaigns
- No keyword segmentation
- Limited optimization history

### Strategy (The Fix)
**Week 1: Full Account Audit**
- Discovered branded keywords performing exceptionally well but buried in bad campaigns
- Identified top 5 best-selling products to focus on
- Archived ineffective keywords and campaigns

**Week 2-3: Restructure**
- Created dedicated branded campaign (exact match)
- Built manual campaigns for top 5 products
- Added negative keywords to prevent overlap
- Set up proper campaign naming convention

**Week 4-8: Optimize**
- Daily bid adjustments based on search term data
- Weekly search term report analysis
- Harvested converting terms from auto to manual
- Optimized listings for higher CVR

**Week 9-12: Scale**
- Expanded to phrase/broad for discovery
- Added product targeting campaigns
- Implemented budget allocation framework (70/20/10)

### After (The Results)
| Metric | Before | After | Change |
|--------|--------|-------|--------|
| Total Sales | Baseline | +48% | ⬆️ |
| ACoS | 93% | ~35% | ⬇️ 62% |
| Ad Spend | Baseline | +39% | ⬆️ (invested more) |
| Profitability | Negative | Positive | ⬆️ |

### Key Lessons
1. High ACoS doesn't always mean "pause everything" — sometimes it means "restructure everything"
2. Branded keywords are gold — don't let them get buried
3. Focus on top products first — don't spread thin
4. 39% more ad spend but 48% more revenue = every dollar working harder

## Case Study 3: Product Launch — 90-Day PPC Playbook

**Category:** Consumables (Kitchen)
**Starting Point:** New product, zero reviews, zero sales history

### Phase 1: Pre-Launch (Days 1-14)
**Actions:**
- Optimized listing: 5 bullet points, A+ Content, 7 images
- Enrolled in Vine program (30 reviews targeted)
- Set up campaign structure:
  - 1 Auto campaign (discovery)
  - 1 Manual Exact campaign (5 high-relevance keywords)
  - 1 Manual Phrase campaign (10 related keywords)
- Budget: $50/day total

**Metrics Target:** Impressions, CTR > 0.3%

### Phase 2: Launch (Days 15-45)
**Actions:**
- Analyzed first 2 weeks of search term data
- Harvested 15 converting terms from auto → manual exact
- Added 30 negative keywords (irrelevant terms)
- Increased bids on top performers by 20%
- Launched Sponsored Brands video (product demo)

**Metrics Target:** CVR > 8%, ACoS < 80% (acceptable for launch)

### Phase 3: Optimization (Days 46-75)
**Actions:**
- Bid optimization: reduced bids on high-ACoS terms, increased on winners
- Dayparting implemented based on conversion data
- Added product targeting (competitor ASINs)
- Budget: increased to $75/day based on results

**Metrics Target:** ACoS < 50%, CVR > 12%

### Phase 4: Scale (Days 76-90)
**Actions:**
- Expanded keyword list to 100+ terms
- Launched Sponsored Display retargeting
- Increased budget to $100/day
- Implemented 70/20/10 allocation

**Metrics Target:** ACoS < 35%, TACoS declining

### Results After 90 Days
| Metric | Day 14 | Day 45 | Day 90 |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Daily Sales | $20 | $150 | $400+ |
| ACoS | N/A | 75% | 30% |
| CVR | 5% | 10% | 16% |
| Reviews | 3 | 18 | 45 |
| Organic Rank (main keyword) | Page 5 | Page 2 | Page 1 |

### Key Lessons
1. Launch phase ACoS is expected to be high — budget for it
2. Listing optimization before ads is non-negotiable
3. Vine reviews are essential for new products
4. 90 days is the minimum for meaningful organic rank improvement
5. Don't scale too fast — let data guide budget increases

## Case Study 4: Q4 Black Friday Campaign

**Category:** Toys
**Budget:** $10,000 for November-December
**Goal:** Maximize revenue during peak season

### October Preparation
- Built 15 new campaigns for seasonal keywords
- Keyword research: "black friday deals [product]", "cyber monday [product]"
- Created campaign structure:
  - Brand defense (10% budget)
  - Product targeting - competitors (20%)
  - Seasonal keywords (40%)
  - Evergreen keywords (30%)
- Set bid baselines at 80% of current levels (room to increase)

### November Execution
**Nov 1-15:** Ramp phase
- Increased daily budget from $150 to $300
- Raised bids by 15% weekly
- Monitored hourly on peak days

**Nov 16-24:** Aggressive phase
- Budget: $500/day
- Bids: +50% on top performers
- Top of Search placement: +75%
- Launched Sponsored Brands video with holiday creative

**Nov 25 (Black Friday):** Peak
- Budget: $1,000/day
- Bids: +100% on proven keywords
- Hourly monitoring
- Real-time bid adjustments

**Nov 26-Dec 1:** Sustain
- Maintained high bids through Cyber Monday
- Started scaling back Dec 2

### December Wind-Down
- Dec 2-15: Gradual budget reduction (20% per week)
- Dec 16-31: Maintenance mode — brand defense only
- Analyzed full Q4 performance

### Results
| Metric | Q4 2025 | Q4 2026 | Change |
|--------|---------|---------|--------|
| Revenue | $25,000 | $62,000 | +148% |
| ACoS | 35% | 22% | ⬇️ 37% |
| Total Ad Spend | $8,750 | $13,640 | +56% |
| ROI | 2.86x | 4.54x | +59% |

### Key Lessons
1. Q4 preparation starts in October — not November
2. Budget needs to be 2-3x normal during peak
3. Hourly monitoring on Black Friday is essential
4. Brand defense during Q4 is critical — competitors are aggressive
5. Post-holiday analysis informs next year's strategy

## Case Study 5: Multi-Product Account Restructuring

**Category:** Home Decor (12 products)
**Starting Point:** 50+ campaigns, no structure, $2,000/month budget

### Problems Identified
1. 50+ campaigns with $1-2/day budgets (too thin)
2. No campaign naming convention
3. Auto and manual campaigns mixed
4. No negative keywords
5. No portfolio organization
6. Same keywords targeted across multiple campaigns

### Restructuring Plan
**Week 1: Audit & Document**
- Cataloged all 50+ campaigns
- Identified 8 dead campaigns (0 impressions in 30 days)
- Identified 12 campaigns with good data
- Created restructuring proposal

**Week 2: Consolidate**
- Merged 50 campaigns → 15 focused campaigns
- Created portfolio structure:
  - Portfolio A: Top 3 sellers (60% budget)
  - Portfolio B: Growth products (30%)
  - Portfolio C: Testing (10%)
- Implemented campaign naming: [Product]_[Type]_[MatchType]

**Week 3: Optimize**
- Built negative keyword lists (shared across campaigns)
- Set up auto → manual harvest flow
- Implemented bid rules based on ACoS targets

**Week 4: Document & Train**
- Created SOP for ongoing management
- Documented all naming conventions
- Set up weekly reporting template

### Results (3 Months Later)
| Metric | Before | After | Change |
|--------|--------|-------|--------|
| Campaigns | 50+ | 15 | ⬇️ 70% |
| Avg. Budget/Campaign | $1.50/day | $4.50/day | ⬆️ 200% |
| ACoS | 55% | 28% | ⬇️ 49% |
| Total Sales | $3,600/mo | $7,100/mo | +97% |
| Time to Manage | 15 hrs/week | 5 hrs/week | ⬇️ 67% |

### Key Lessons
1. More campaigns ≠ better performance
2. Minimum $10/day per campaign for meaningful data
3. Naming conventions save hours of confusion
4. Shared negative keyword lists are essential
5. Structure first, optimize second

## Case Study 6: Competitor Conquesting Strategy

**Category:** Cosmetics
**Goal:** steal market share from top 3 competitors

### Strategy
**Phase 1: Intelligence Gathering**
- Identified top 3 competitor ASINs by search volume
- Analyzed their listings: price, reviews, images, bullets
- Found gaps: they had weak main images, no video

**Phase 2: Product Targeting Campaigns**
- Created Sponsored Products product targeting campaigns
- Targeted each competitor ASIN individually
- Set bids 20% above their estimated CPC
- Created compelling ad copy (via Sponsored Brands)

**Phase 3: Defensive Optimization**
- Launched brand defense campaigns on own brand terms
- Created Sponsored Brands headline targeting competitor brand terms
- Built A+ Content comparing features (without naming competitors)

**Phase 4: Measurement**
- Tracked: impressions, clicks, conversions from competitor targeting
- Measured: new customer rate from conquesting campaigns
- Monitored: organic ranking improvement from increased velocity

### Results (6 Months)
| Metric | Before | After | Change |
|--------|--------|-------|--------|
| Market Share | 5% | 12% | +140% |
| Competitor #1 Reviews | 2,500 | 2,300 (they lost some) | ⬇️ |
| New Customer % | 30% | 55% | +83% |
| Organic Rank (main keyword) | #8 | #3 | ⬆️ |

### Key Lessons
1. Competitor targeting works — but requires patience (6+ months)
2. Better listing beats higher bids — optimize first
3. Brand defense is essential when conquesting — protect your flank
4. New customer metric proves conquesting is working
5. Don't name competitors in content — focus on your strengths
